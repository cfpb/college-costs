What This Tool Is About and Who It's For

Getting accepted to college is a big cause for celebration — a point of pride for both students and their families. But the prospect of financing a college education can sometimes be overwhelming.

This April, over 1.5 million students and their families will be sifting through over 5 million college acceptances. To help students and families make smarter choices about college financing, we are testing a prototype of a new College Cost Comparison Worksheet — an interactive, web-based tool that enables students and families to make side-by-side cost comparisons between schools, tailored to their unique financial circumstances and actual costs of attendance.

The school financial aid offer letter is one of the most important ways students receive this information. But we’ve heard from students, college counselors, and community organizations that many of these financial aid offer letters don’t always effectively deliver this information.

A few months ago, we worked with the Department of Education to release a prototype “financial aid shopping sheet” to make financial aid offer letters easier to understand. Tens of thousands of Americans took a look, and we received terrific feedback.

This is just the first step in the process of developing a tool that helps students and parents make smarter choices. Your feedback is an important part of this process, and we know that we have a ways to go in order to come up with a great product.

Why We Built This Tool

The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau is a 21st Century Agency committed to using technology to empower consumers to take control over their economic lives.

Figuring out how to finance an education can be daunting. This may be an important reason why some students are choosing higher-priced private loans before exhausting subsidized federal loan options. Some students and their families resort to credit cards and other debt products when better options are available.

With the College Cost Comparison Worksheet, students and their families can compare important information before they make their final college selection—helping families make smarter choices about college cost and student debt.

How This Tool Works

We wanted to provide a tool that aggregates existing data from various sources to help you make decisions during this financial aid season.
